What “agentic” means

Agentic AI is a pattern where the model doesn’t just answer once. It can plan steps, call tools, and iterate until it completes a goal.

Where agents help (and where they don’t)

Agents shine when work involves multiple steps: searching, transforming, checking, and producing a final output. They struggle when the task is unclear, the tools are unreliable, or there is no way to verify correctness.

The reliability checklist

Use clear goal + constraints

Break work into small steps

Add tool-result validation

Use timeouts and limits

Log what the agent did

Key takeaway

Agentic AI works best when you treat it like an automation pipeline with guardrails—not magic.
